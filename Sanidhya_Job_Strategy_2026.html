04 / Outreach Strategy

The Daily Execution Plan

Consistency beats intensity. 5 targeted emails beat 50 copy-paste blasts every time.

01

Daily Email Volume

  • → Send 3–5 cold emails per day — no more. Quality cap is essential.
  • → Research each company for 10 min before writing. Find one real hook.
  • → Monday–Friday only. Emails sent Tue–Thu 9–11 AM get highest open rates.
  • → Keep a spreadsheet: Company | Contact | Email sent | Follow-up date | Status
02

LinkedIn Strategy

  • → Optimize profile first: headline = "AI/ML + SWE | CMRIT '27 | YOLOv5 · CNN · React"
  • → Send 5–7 LinkedIn connection requests per day to engineers, CTOs, TAs at target companies
  • → After connecting, wait 2 days then send a short DM (max 3 lines + ask)
  • → Post 1× per week: a breakdown of your project, a lesson learnt, a problem you solved
  • → Comment meaningfully on posts by people at target companies — visibility is free
03

Platform Applications

  • → Internshala: apply to 3–5 listings every morning (set alerts for Python + ML + BLR)
  • → Wellfound (AngelList): best for startup roles; message hiring managers directly after applying
  • → Naukri.com: keep profile updated with all keywords — recruiters search it daily
  • → Unstop: hackathons + hiring challenges — winning one changes your trajectory
  • → GitHub: pin both projects, add GIFs/demo screenshots to READMEs — this is your portfolio
04

Resume Matching

  • → AI/DS Resume → CV roles, ML intern, AI researcher, surveillance tech, KYC/fintech AI
  • → SWE Resume → Full-stack intern, product SDE, backend engineer, generalist startup engineer
  • → Read the JD carefully — match 3–4 keywords from it into your email naturally
  • → Never send the PDF resume version to startups — send the clean DOCX or a link
05 / Follow-up Timeline

When to Follow Up — Exactly

Most placements happen on the 2nd or 3rd touchpoint. Never ghost your own outreach.

Day 0
Send the cold email. Attach the correct resume. Double-check name spelling. Send Tue–Thu, 9–11 AM.
Day 1
Connect on LinkedIn with the same person (or HR) — friendly note: "Hi, I sent you an email yesterday about the [role]. Just connecting here too."
Day 5–6
First follow-up email. Keep it under 4 lines. "Hi [Name], just bumping this up. Happy to share my project demo or hop on a quick call. Thanks!"
Day 12
Final follow-up. Try a different angle — lead with a project update, a new metric, or something relevant to their company news. One line offer: "If timing isn't right, happy to reconnect in [Month]."
Day 14+
Archive and move on. Mark in your tracker. Do not follow up a 4th time. Re-approach in 2–3 months if the company is still a priority.
LinkedIn DM
3-line formula: 1) One genuine observation about them/their work. 2) One sentence on who you are + strongest proof point. 3) A specific, easy ask ("Would a 15-min call make sense?" not "Please give me a job").
06 / Mindset Rules

What Separates Placed from Passed Over

The technical bar is the minimum. These habits decide the outcome.

✦ DO THESE

  • Mention a specific number in every email (94%, 40%, 25ms)
  • Personalise the first line — show you've done 10 minutes of research
  • Include your GitHub link — let the code speak
  • Follow up exactly on schedule — persistence signals seriousness
  • Keep your LinkedIn "Open to Work" visible to recruiters only
  • Post your project breakdowns on LinkedIn — organic inbound happens
  • Apply to 3 companies in the same sector the same week (warm memory effect)
  • Track every outreach in a spreadsheet — you're running a pipeline

✦ AVOID THESE

  • Don't send generic "I am interested in opportunities" emails
  • Don't attach a 3-page resume — 1 page, tailored, every time
  • Don't apply to FAANG as your first priority — fix rate is near 0 for freshers cold-applying
  • Don't copy-paste the same email to 20 companies in one day
  • Don't mention CGPA unless it's above 8.5 or they specifically ask
  • Don't write "I am a quick learner" — show it through project metrics instead
  • Don't follow up more than 3 times — it turns from persistence to pestering
  • Don't ignore Wellfound/Unstop — they're where startups actually hire from
